Top Ten Times The Simpsons
Predicted The Future

The Simpsons has eerily predicted real-world events many times during its long run.
Explore the top 10 instances when the show seemed to foresee the future.


Pandemic & Killer Bees

"Marge in Chains" (1993)

In the episode, a mysterious Asian virus called "The Osaka Flu" spreads to Springfield, sparking a pandemic resembling the COVID-19 pandemic of 2020. To add to the uncanny similarities, the episode concludes with a mob scene outside a hospital that topples a truck containing "Killer Bees," reminiscent of the "Murder Hornets" that arrived towards the end of the COVID-19 lockdowns.

Homer Hammers Out the God Particle

"The Wizard of Evergreen Terrace" (1998)

Homer becomes an inventor and is seen working on a blackboard creating a complicated equation that eerily resembles the findings of the Higgs Boson particle, otherwise known as the "God Particle," discovered in 2012, an intriguing hint at scientific breakthroughs. The "God Particle" is responsible for granting other particles their mass, revolutionizing our understanding of the universe.

Donald Trump Becomes President

"Bart to the Future" (2000)

Lisa Simpson becomes president, charged with restoring order and mending the nation after a disastrous presidency by Trump in an episode that aired more than 15 years before his 2016 candidacy announcement.

A Doughnut-Shaped Universe

"They Saved Lisa's Brain" (1999)

Homer presents a theory to Stephen Hawking that the universe is donut-shaped. Four years later cosmologists observed radiation patterns indicating the universe might be tubular and round, with a "hole" in the middle resembling a donut.

Siegfried & Roy Tiger Attack

"$pringfield (Or, How I Learned to Stop Worrying and Love Legalized Gambling)" (1993)

In the episode, a white tiger attacks "Gunter and Ernst," a parody of Siegfried and Roy, during their act as revenge for being snatched from the wild. Nearly a decade later, Roy experienced a career-ending injury in 2003 when a tiger attacked him during a live performance.

The Shard Highrise in London

"Lisa's Wedding" (1995)

In the episode, Lisa travels to London and observes a tall, pointed building that wasn't typically part of the London skyline, foreshadowing future urban developments. Fourteen years later, in the exact location depicted in the show, The Shard Skyscraper is constructed, named "The Shard" due to its shard-like appearance, becoming the tallest skyscraper in the UK.

Smart Watches & Video Calls

"Lisa's Wedding" (1995)

A fortune-teller reveals Lisa's future, where she uses technology that did not exist at the time, including a watch-phone and video chat-enabled phones, astonishingly predicting advancements ahead of their time. The episode aired 20 years before the Apple Watch's release and 15 years before the introduction of FaceTime, showcasing uncanny foresight.

NSA Spying on American Citizens

"The Simpsons Movie" (2007)

The Simpson family becomes fugitives, and Lisa worries about surveillance but is dismissed and assured the government isn't listening to everyone's conversations. The scene later reveals the NSA is tracking their conversations. In 2013, Edward Snowden exposed the NSA's mass surveillance activities including phone calls, texts, emails, social media messages, and web browsing.

Greece Bankruptcy

"Politically Inept" (2012)

During Homer's appearance on the news, there is an alert running at the bottom of the screen that reads, "Europe puts Greece on eBay." In 2015, Greece faced a significant debt crisis and the country nearly went bankrupt until securing a bailout from Europe.

Underwater Submersible Emergency

"Homer's Paternity Coot" (2006)

Homer attempts to recover treasure from a sunken ship when he gets stuck and his lack of oxygen leads to a coma. In 2023, an incident involved an OceanGate submersible disappearing while exploring the Titanic wreck, tragically imploding with no survivors.
